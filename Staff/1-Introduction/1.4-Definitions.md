# 1.4 Definitions

## 1.4.1 Purpose

This document defines the official meanings of terms used throughout the Staff Documentation.

These definitions apply to all staff policies, procedures, and decisions.

---

# 1.4.2 Staff

**Staff** refers to any individual who has been granted a server position with moderation, administrative, support, or operational responsibilities.

Staff members are expected to follow all policies within this documentation regardless of rank.

---

# 1.4.3 Management

**Management** refers to staff members with authority to make organizational decisions, review staff performance, approve changes, and oversee server operations.

Management includes any role specifically assigned leadership authority such as admin.

---

# 1.4.4 Member

**Member** refers to any user who participates within the server community without official staff responsibilities.

Members are subject to the same server rules and policies as all other users.

---

# 1.4.5 Staff Member

**Staff Member** refers to an individual currently holding an assigned staff position.

A staff member is responsible for completing assigned duties and maintaining the standards outlined in this handbook.

---

# 1.4.6 Staff Permission

**Staff Permission** refers to any access, command, role ability, or administrative tool provided to a staff member.

Staff permissions may only be used for authorized server-related purposes.

---

# 1.4.7 Authority

**Authority** refers to the approved ability of a staff member to perform actions based on their assigned role.

Authority is limited by rank, responsibility, and server policy.

---

# 1.4.8 Incident

**Incident** refers to any situation requiring staff attention, investigation, moderation action, or documentation.

Examples include:

- Rule violations.
- Member conflicts.
- Security concerns.
- Staff-related issues.

---

# 1.4.9 Evidence

**Evidence** refers to any information used to support a staff decision.

Examples include:

- Screenshots.
- Message logs.
- Recordings.
- Server logs.
- Witness statements.

Staff members must not issue serious punishments without sufficient evidence unless immediate action is required.

---

# 1.4.10 Escalation

**Escalation** refers to transferring an issue to a higher-ranking staff member for review or decision-making.

Escalation is required when:

- A staff member lacks permission to handle the situation.
- The situation exceeds their experience.
- A conflict of interest exists.
- The situation involves another staff member.

---

# 1.4.11 Disciplinary Action

**Disciplinary Action** refers to any corrective action taken due to policy violations.

Examples include:

- Verbal warning.
- Written warning.
- Removal of permissions.
- Demotion.
- Removal from staff.

---

# 1.4.12 Policy

**Policy** refers to an official rule, requirement, or procedure established by server management.

All staff members are required to follow active policies.

---

# 1.4.13 Procedure

**Procedure** refers to an approved method for completing a specific task or handling a situation.

Staff members must follow established procedures unless authorized otherwise.

---

# 1.4.14 Confidential Information

**Confidential Information** refers to any internal staff information that is not intended for public access.

Examples include:

- Staff discussions.
- Reports.
- Internal decisions.
- Private logs.

Confidential information must not be shared without authorization.
