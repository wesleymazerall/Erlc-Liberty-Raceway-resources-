# 1.3 Chain of Command

## 1.3.1 Purpose

This document defines the authority structure, reporting process, and communication hierarchy of the Staff Team.

The Chain of Command exists to ensure decisions are handled properly and responsibilities are clearly assigned.

All staff members must follow the Chain of Command unless an emergency situation requires immediate action.

---

## 1.3.2 Staff Hierarchy

Staff authority follows the established rank structure.

Higher-ranking staff members have additional responsibilities and decision-making authority.

Example structure:

1. Owner / Executive Management
2. Administration
3. Senior Moderation
4. Moderation
5. Support / Trial Staff

Rank names may differ depending on server structure, but authority must always follow the approved hierarchy.

---

## 1.3.3 Authority Limits

Staff members may only perform actions authorized by their rank and assigned responsibilities.

Staff members must not:

- Override decisions made by higher-ranking staff without approval.
- Use permissions outside their assigned role.
- Give orders to staff members outside their authority level.
- Modify server systems without authorization.

---

## 1.3.4 Reporting Structure

Staff members must report issues through the appropriate chain.

The correct process is:

1. Report the issue to the next available higher-ranking staff member.
2. Provide all relevant information and evidence.
3. Wait for guidance if the situation requires approval.

Staff members must not bypass the Chain of Command unless:

- A higher-ranking staff member is unavailable.
- The issue involves abuse of authority.
- Immediate action is required to protect the server.

---

## 1.3.5 Respect for Authority

All staff members are required to respect legitimate decisions made by authorized staff.

Disagreements must be handled privately and professionally.

Staff members must not:

- Publicly challenge another staff member's decision.
- Create conflict between staff members.
- Encourage members to ignore staff decisions.

---

## 1.3.6 Leadership Responsibilities

Higher-ranking staff members are responsible for:

- Providing guidance to lower-ranking staff.
- Reviewing staff actions when necessary.
- Ensuring policies are followed.
- Supporting staff development.

Leadership positions are based on responsibility and trust, not personal privilege.

---

## 1.3.7 Escalation

If a staff member is unsure how to handle a situation, they must escalate the issue to a higher-ranking staff member.

Making an incorrect decision due to uncertainty when guidance was available may result in review.
