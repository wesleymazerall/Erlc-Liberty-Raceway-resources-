# 2.3 Inactivity

## 2.3.1 Purpose

This document defines the standards for identifying, reviewing, and handling staff inactivity.

Maintaining an active Staff Team is required to ensure server operations remain effective and members continue receiving proper support.

---

# 2.3.2 Definition of Inactivity

A staff member is considered inactive when they fail to participate in required staff responsibilities without an approved Leave of Absence (LOA).

Inactivity may include:

- Not performing assigned staff duties.
- Not responding to required staff communications.
- Not participating in moderation or support activities.
- Failing to complete assigned tasks.
- Being unavailable for an extended period without notice.

Simply remaining a member of the server does not count as staff activity.

---

# 2.3.3 Unapproved Inactivity

Any period of inactivity without an approved LOA is considered unapproved inactivity.

Unapproved inactivity may result in:

- Activity review.
- Staff warning.
- Removal of responsibilities.
- Rank reduction.
- Removal from the Staff Team.

---

# 2.3.4 Activity Review Process

When inactivity is identified, management may perform an activity review.

The review may include:

- Staff activity history.
- Communication records.
- Completed responsibilities.
- Previous warnings.
- LOA history.

Management determines whether further action is required.

---

# 2.3.5 Required Communication

Staff members who are unable to complete their responsibilities must communicate through the proper channels.

Staff members must not assume that inactivity will be automatically understood or excused.

Failure to communicate does not remove responsibility.

---

# 2.3.6 Return After Inactivity

A staff member returning after an inactive period may be required to:

- Confirm availability.
- Review missed updates.
- Resume assigned responsibilities.
- Complete any required re-training.

Management may determine whether the staff member returns to their previous responsibilities.

---

# 2.3.7 Repeated Inactivity

Repeated periods of inactivity may result in additional review.

A pattern of unreliable activity may indicate that the staff member is unable to maintain the requirements of their position.

Repeated inactivity may result in:

- Permanent removal from staff.
- Loss of rank.
- Removal of permissions.

---

# 2.3.8 Management Authority

Management has final authority regarding activity requirements and staff status.

Management decisions regarding inactivity reviews are based on operational needs, staff performance, and documented activity.
