# 2.2 Leave of Absence

## 2.2.1 Purpose

This document defines the requirements and procedures for requesting an approved Leave of Absence (LOA).

The LOA system exists to allow staff members to temporarily step away from duties while maintaining proper communication and accountability.

---

# 2.2.2 LOA Requirement

Any staff member who expects to be unavailable for an extended period is required to submit an LOA request.

An LOA request must be submitted before the period of inactivity begins unless an emergency prevents prior submission.

Failure to submit an LOA request may result in the absence being treated as unapproved inactivity.

---

# 2.2.3 LOA Submission Process

All LOA requests must be submitted through the **Melonly Moderator Panel** using the official LOA request system.

The request must contain:

- Staff member name.
- Current staff position.
- Date the absence begins.
- Expected return date.
- Reason for absence (optional if private).
- Any information needed for duty coverage.

Requests submitted outside of the official system may not be considered valid.

---

# 2.2.4 LOA Approval

An LOA is only considered active after approval from authorized staff.

Submission of an LOA request does not automatically grant approval.

Management reserves the right to:

- Approve requests.
- Request additional information.
- Modify the approved duration.
- Deny requests if necessary.

---

# 2.2.5 During an Approved LOA

While on an approved LOA, staff members:

- Are temporarily excused from normal activity requirements.
- Must not perform staff duties unless authorized.
- Must remain aware of their expected return date.

An approved LOA does not remove a staff member's responsibility to follow staff policies.

---

# 2.2.6 LOA Extensions

If additional time is required beyond the approved return date, the staff member must submit an updated LOA request.

Extensions must be submitted before the original LOA expires.

Failure to request an extension may result in the staff member being marked inactive.

---

# 2.2.7 Returning From LOA

Upon returning from an approved LOA, the staff member is expected to resume normal staff responsibilities.

The staff member may be required to:

- Review missed staff information.
- Confirm availability.
- Resume assigned duties.

---

# 2.2.8 Abuse of LOA System

The LOA system must not be used to avoid staff responsibilities or prevent accountability.

Examples of LOA abuse include:

- Repeated unnecessary LOA requests.
- Remaining inactive after LOA expiration.
- Providing false information.

Abuse of the LOA system may result in disciplinary action.
