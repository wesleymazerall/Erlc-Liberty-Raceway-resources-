# 6.2 Escalation

## 6.2.1 Purpose

This document defines when and how ticket issues should be escalated to higher-ranking staff.

---

## 6.2.2 When Escalation Is Required

Staff members must escalate situations when:

- They do not have the required permissions.
- The situation exceeds their authority.
- The issue involves another staff member.
- The issue requires management approval.
- They are unsure how to proceed.

---

## 6.2.3 Escalation Process

Staff members must:

1. Provide the relevant information.
2. Explain actions already taken.
3. Provide evidence when available.
4. Wait for further direction if required.

---

## 6.2.4 Improper Escalation

Staff members must not:

- Ignore situations requiring escalation.
- Escalate issues unnecessarily to avoid responsibility.
- Bypass the Chain of Command.

---

## 6.2.5 Final Decisions

Higher-ranking staff members may make final decisions on escalated issues within their authority.
